About Muons

Muons are elementary particles similar to electrons, but with a much greater mass. They are produced naturally in the upper atmosphere when cosmic rays interact with atomic nuclei. Due to their high energy and relatively long lifetime, muons can penetrate deep into materials, making them useful for scientific research and practical applications.

Detection Mechanics

Muons are typically detected using devices such as scintillation counters, drift chambers, or Cherenkov detectors. When a muon passes through these detectors, it interacts with the material, producing light or electrical signals. These signals are then processed to determine the presence, trajectory, and energy of the muon, enabling researchers to study their properties and origins.
