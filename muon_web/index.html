About the Project

We have built a muon detector at the University of Rochester Department of Physics and Astronomy. Our goal is to collect cosmic ray muon data and make it accessible for analysis by high school students and educators.

Our Detector

The detector is designed to measure the flux of muons at the Earth's surface. It uses scintillator panels and photomultiplier tubes to detect passing muons, with data acquisition handled by custom electronics.

Physics Background

Muons are elementary particles produced when cosmic rays interact with the Earth's atmosphere. Studying muons helps us understand particle physics, cosmic rays, and atmospheric processes.
